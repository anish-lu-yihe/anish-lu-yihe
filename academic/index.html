Profile
I am currently in the final year of my Ph.D. study under the supervision of Dr. Yulia Timofeeva at the Centre for Complexity Science. The Ph.D. project is aimed at understanding the modulation of dendritic functions by their structures. The thesis is completed and will be submitted soon.

Recent Work
Neuronal dendrites branch and taper. The complexity of branching tree structures has fascinated scientists since the exemplary work of Ramón y Cajal, whilst the tapered structures have been considered as a secondary feature. To address their effects, Poznanski has established a set of useful transformations that allow some tapered cable equations to be solved analytically. Based on that, we have generalised the sum-over-trips framework for computing response functions on branching structures with cylindrical cables to a method capable of acquiring response functions for an arbitrary dendritic tree with tapered branches. This gives us a better understanding of the role of tapered dendritic morphologies on signal processing and dendritic integration. A working paper is to be submitted soon.
